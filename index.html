background

My animating passion is for exploration and to discover new connections and patterns in the world around us. After studying Music History at Reed College, I started working in Portland, Oregon area non-profits, completing successful terms in administrative and operations roles at an organization dedicated to youth and family services, then performing arts. In March 2020, I was an essential part of pivoting the Portland Baroque Orchestra to an all-digital season, both as part of an administrative team and directly as a recording assistant and video editor. In autumn 2021, I left PBO to pursue more challenging and intellectually stimulating work. I am pursuing work on the path toward a career change to software and web development.

career change

When I was going through high school, it was still a new idea that you could submit school work electronically. One of my teachers still accepted handwritten papers! Facebook arrived on campus sophomore year, and we all could see that the internet was going to change everything but coding wasn't yet treated as a skill that everyone could learn. Java was offered as an elective, but only if you had finished calculus. I was not a math loving teenager, and I was never going to make it in the door of a calculus class, let alone finish one. So I never thought that computer programming was a realistic career path.

In 2019, I took an introductory programming class in Portland, and that experience opened me up to the idea that I could be successful as a software or web developer. It took me a couple more years to figure out how to attend a full time learning program. The COVID-19 crisis was the straw that broke the camel's back. My job description was shifting all the time and I was ready to take a big leap into change. I am very excited to find out what happens next.
